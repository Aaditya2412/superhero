Spider-Man’s Iconic Villains

Spider-Man’s journey wouldn’t be complete without his incredible rogue’s gallery of villains. From scientific geniuses turned evil to alien symbiotes, these characters have become legends in their own right. Below is a list of Spider-Man’s most notorious enemies.

1. Green Goblin (Norman Osborn)

Norman Osborn, the wealthy and brilliant CEO of Oscorp, became Spider-Man's arch-nemesis as the Green Goblin. Armed with advanced technology, including his iconic glider and pumpkin bombs, the Goblin is responsible for some of Spider-Man’s greatest tragedies—including the death of Gwen Stacy.

2. Doctor Octopus (Otto Octavius)

Otto Octavius, a brilliant scientist, became Doctor Octopus after a lab accident fused four mechanical arms to his body. His intelligence and determination to defeat Spider-Man have made him a recurring foe, even temporarily taking over Peter Parker’s body in the *Superior Spider-Man* storyline.

3. Venom (Eddie Brock)

Venom is the result of a bond between Eddie Brock and a sentient alien symbiote. With abilities similar to Spider-Man, Venom is both a dangerous enemy and, at times, an antihero. His hatred for Spider-Man stems from their shared history with the symbiote.

4. Sandman (Flint Marko)

Flint Marko, also known as the Sandman, gained the ability to transform his body into sand after a freak accident. His tragic backstory as a small-time criminal who turns to crime to support his daughter adds depth to his character.

5. The Lizard (Dr. Curt Connors)

Dr. Curt Connors, a scientist and mentor to Peter Parker, became the Lizard after experimenting with reptilian DNA. While he retains his intelligence in some versions, his monstrous alter ego is driven by primal instincts, often putting Spider-Man in difficult moral dilemmas.
